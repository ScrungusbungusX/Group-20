Case 3

Lost in Encryption

You are in a front parlor room. The window is boarded shut. There is a dim light emitting from a lamp in the corner. The room contains a single door.


You attempt to open the door. It is locked. Through searching the room you find a drawer. When opened you find a key. After attempting to open the door with the key it is still locked.


After further examination there is a piece of paper in the drawer. Written on the paper is a series of sentences that you cannot interpret.


You come to realise the lines of text on the paper are a series of intructions. When these instructions are executed the door unlocks.


The written text has been encrypted using a caesar cipher. In order to read and interpret the sentences you must crack the cipher.


A Caesar Cipher is an encryption method in which the text/characters have been shifted forward or backwards by a fixed number of positions.

A simple way to crack the cipher and find how many places the characters have been shifted is to identify the single letter words.

In the English language there is only 2 one letter words, these being 'i' and 'a'.

Then count the positions and use trial and error to find the correct key and decipher the text.

Use the alphabet below to help count the number of shifts. You can also use a pen and paper or the provided textbox.
