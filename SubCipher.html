Substitution Ciphers

A substitution cipher is when a message or 'plaintext' is encrypted by taking each letter in the message and substituting it with a different letter or symbol. The English alphabet has 26 letters, this may not sound like very many at first glance, if I asked you to guess which letter I'm currently thinking of you would have a 1/26 chance of guessing correctly but what about if I asked you to guess a word? or a sentence?

Happily we don't need to rely purely on guesswork. You may have noticed that the encrypted message we found in Miss Earnshaws sitting room has some repeating characters...

We can see a second double matching this description but with a different symbol. Let's make an educated guess of 'E' for the pink tulip and 'O' for the purple.

Sticking with our current guesses it seems likely that the pink tulip will be a consonant. As it appears frequently (3 times) in a short sentence we might guess it's a more commonly occuring consonant. It also appears in a two letter word. As we have already guessed 'o' and 'e' it cannot be a 2 letter word containing this letter, the most likely options this leaves us with are: 'as', 'an', 'at', 'if', 'in'.

4 Letter words ending 'ees': BEES.
4 Letter words ending 'een': BEEN, TEEN, KEEN, SEEN.
4 Letter words ending 'eet' : MEET, FEET, BEET
4 Letter words ending 'eef': BEEF, REEF

Based on the options above 'MEET' seems most likely so we can guess the pink tulip is a 'T'
